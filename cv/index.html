    Structures and process-level lexical interactions in memory search: A case study of individuals with cochlear implants and normal hearing
    In Proceedings of the 46th Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society
    Searching through memory is mediated by complex interactions between the underlying mental lexicon and the processes that operate on this lexicon. However, these interactions are difficult to study due to the effortless manner in which neurotypical individuals perform cognitive tasks.
